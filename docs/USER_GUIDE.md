## 1. Odontogram — Recording Tooth Conditions

### How to open the odontogram

1. Go to a patient's record.
2. In the Quick Links on the right, click **Odontogram**.
3. You will see the full tooth chart — upper arch on top, lower arch on the bottom, drawn in a gentle curve like a real mouth. Each tooth is shaped to match its type (incisor, canine, premolar, molar).

### How the chart looks and behaves

- **Anatomical tooth shapes** — incisors are narrow and chisel-shaped, canines are pointed, premolars are rounded, molars are wide. You can recognise each tooth at a glance.
- **Curved upper and lower arches** — back teeth sit slightly behind front teeth, just like in the mouth.
- **Soft entrance animation** — when the page opens, teeth fan into place one after another (very quick — under half a second total).
- **Hover lift** — when you move your finger or mouse over a tooth, it gently lifts and gets a subtle shadow so you know it's the active one.
- **Selected tooth pulses** — a blue ring softly pulses around the tooth you're working on.
- **No constant motion** — once a tooth is recorded, nothing keeps moving. Animation only happens when you interact.

### How to record a condition on a tooth

1. **Tap any tooth** in the chart. The tooth **zooms out of the arch** into a large, centred editor — the surrounding chart dims and blurs behind it. The whole zoom takes under half a second.
2. In the focus editor you will see:
   - A **big anatomically realistic tooth** — incisors are chisel-shaped, canines pointed, premolars rounded with twin cusps, molars wide with four cusps and grooves.
   - The crown is divided into **five tappable surfaces**: Buccal (top), Mesial (left), Occlusal/Incisal (centre), Distal (right), Lingual (bottom). A separate **Cervical** band sits at the gumline.
   - On the right: **Tooth Status** (one), **Findings** (multi-select, grouped by clinical level), and a **Clear surface** button when a surface is active.
3. **Tap a surface** to select it. It fills with the current status colour. Tap again to clear it. The whole-tooth colour is used when no specific surface is chosen.
4. **Tap a status** (Healthy / Caries / Deep Caries / Filled / Missing / Crown / RCT / Implant). The colour transitions in smoothly — the tooth never snaps. Decorations like the RCT cross, crown ring, or implant screw appear over the morphology.
5. **Tap findings** to add or remove them. They appear as overlays on the big tooth (calculus band at the gumline, stains as brown stipple, gingival recession as a pink dashed line, etc.).
6. All changes are **saved automatically** — no Save button.
7. **Close the editor** by pressing **Esc**, tapping the **X**, or tapping the dimmed area outside the tooth. The tooth zooms back into its exact position in the arch.

### Numbering systems — FDI or Universal

In the top-right of the chart there is a small toggle: **FDI** / **Universal**.

- **FDI** (default) — the international 2-digit system used in India (11–48 for adults, 51–85 for pediatric).
- **Universal** — the American system (1–32 for adults, A–T for pediatric). Useful if a referring clinic uses Universal numbering.

The system always stores tooth records in FDI internally; the toggle only changes what number is shown on the chart label.

### Keyboard navigation (tablet keyboard or laptop)

- **Tab** — move focus into the chart, onto the first tooth.
- **Arrow keys** — move between teeth (Left / Right across a row, Up / Down between upper and lower arch).
- **Enter** or **Space** — zoom the currently focused tooth into the focus editor.
- **Esc** — close the focus editor; focus returns to the same tooth in the arch.
- Inside the focus editor: **Tab** cycles through status / findings / surface buttons; **Enter/Space** selects the focused control; surfaces can be activated by keyboard too.

### Touch-friendly + accessible

- Tap targets are large, the hover lift also fires on touch focus, and there is no idle motion that would distract during consultation.
- If the operating system is set to **Reduce Motion** (Accessibility setting), all entrance, pulse, and hover-scale animations are turned off automatically. The chart remains fully functional.

> For **pediatric patients** (under 12 years old), the chart automatically switches to the primary (baby) tooth layout (teeth 51–85, or A–T in Universal).

## 2. Odontogram — New Dental Findings

### Primary tooth statuses (choose one per tooth)

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Healthy | No pathology — normal tooth |
| Caries | Cavity present |
| **Deep Caries** | Extensive cavity, close to pulp |
| Filled | Tooth has a restoration |
| Missing | Tooth is absent |
| Crown | Tooth is crowned |
| RCT | Root canal treated |
| Implant | Implant placed (shows screw icon) |

### Additional findings (multi-select — can choose several on one tooth)

These appear in the **Findings** section of the picker. Each is grouped by clinical level:

| Finding | Clinical Level | What it means |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| **Stains** | Tooth | Extrinsic or intrinsic staining on the crown |
| **Calculus** | Gingival / Site | Calculus (tartar) deposit at the gingival margin |
| **Gingival Recession** | Gingival / Site | Recession of the gum tissue around this tooth |
| **Cervical Abrasion** | Tooth | Wedge-shaped notch at the cervical (neck) area |
| **Crowding** | Arch / Region | Tooth is crowded or rotated within the arch |

**How to add a finding:**
1. Click a tooth to open the picker.
2. Under "Findings", click the finding you want (it highlights with a ring).
3. Click more if needed — findings stack.
4. To remove a finding, click it again to deselect.

## 3. Odontogram — Surface Selection

The **Surface** section in the picker shows 6 surfaces with full names. Tap the surface that is affected:

| Surface Name | Code | Used for |
|-------------|------|----------|
| Mesial | M | Surface facing the midline |
| Distal | D | Surface facing away from midline |
| Occlusal/Incisal | O | Biting surface (posterior) / cutting edge (anterior) |
| Buccal | B | Surface facing the cheek |
| Lingual/Palatal | L | Surface facing the tongue or palate |
| Cervical | C | Surface at the neck of the tooth, near the gum |

**Tips:**
- Surface is optional — record it when the condition is on a specific surface (e.g., mesial caries, buccal calculus).
- Tap the same surface again to deselect it.
- Good sized touch targets — works comfortably on a tablet during consultation.

## 4. Odontogram — Reading the Chart

### Realistic visual treatment for every status

Each tooth is rendered with a **glossy enamel base** and a status-specific visual treatment — shape, texture, symbol, and colour — so differences are unmistakable at a glance. Every status also has a unique shape or letter marker so the chart is readable even in colour-blind mode.

| Status | What you see on the tooth |
|--------|--------------------------|
| **Healthy** | Clean white ivory tooth with a soft gloss highlight. Thin green outline. Nothing else. |
| **Caries** | Amber-yellow tooth with a **small dark-brown circle** on the affected surface — the cavity lesion. |
| **Deep Caries** | Orange-red tooth with a **large dark radial stain** (brown at the edges, black at the centre, with a black pit) eating into the tooth. Clearly bigger and darker than regular Caries. |
| **Filled** | Blue-tinted tooth with a **silver-grey amalgam patch** (metallic rectangle with a highlight stripe) on the restoration surface. |
| **Missing** | Faded/ghosted tooth body at low opacity + a **bold dark-red X** across the whole tooth. |
| **Crown** | Warm porcelain-ivory body with a stronger gloss sheen + a thin cervical rim line + a **violet inner ring** outlining the cap. |
| **RCT** | Soft pink-tinted tooth + **red canal lines** drawn down the root (one line for incisors/canines, two for premolars/molars). |
| **Implant** | Ivory crown + a **grey metallic titanium screw** in the root area — V-shaped thread lines visible, metallic sheen gradient. |

**In the zoom-focus editor** all treatments appear at 5× scale with more detail: deep caries shows a clear black pit, the amalgam patch fills the whole surface, canal lines run the full root length, and the implant screw has 7 thread sets with a highlight stripe.

### Surface selection shows where on the tooth

When you select a surface in the picker, a **semi-transparent blue dashed patch** appears on the tooth at the anatomical surface location:

- **Occlusal/Incisal** — top band of the crown (chewing edge)
- **Cervical** — narrow band at the gum-line (CEJ)
- **Mesial** — left side strip (toward midline)
- **Distal** — right side strip (away from midline)
- **Buccal/Lingual** — centre face block

Tap the same surface again to clear it.

### Finding overlays (always drawn on top of the status)

| Overlay | What you see |
|---------|-------------|
| Stains | 3 brown dots in the top-right of the crown |
| Calculus | Solid yellow-brown band at the gingival margin |
| Gingival Recession | Pink dashed line just below the gum line |
| Cervical Abrasion | Cyan V-notch at the cervical (neck) area |
| Crowding | Violet inward-pointing chevrons at the left and right edges |

All overlays are drawn at the **anatomically correct end** of the tooth — upper-arch teeth have gingival overlays at the bottom of the SVG (next to the midline gap), lower-arch teeth at the top.

### Legend

Below the chart there are two legend rows:
- **Status** — all 8 primary statuses with their colours and glyph labels (e.g. `Caries [C]`)
- **Findings** — all 5 findings with their colours and the clinical level label (tooth / gingival / arch)

## 5. Billing — Draft vs Issued Invoice

### Overview of the invoice lifecycle

```
[Create Draft] → [Review & Edit] → [Finalize] → [Record Payment] → [Paid]
```

A new invoice always starts as a **Draft**. It is **not counted** in revenue, receivables, or analytics until it is **finalized**.

### Step 1 — Create an invoice (saved as Draft)

1. Go to **Billing** → click **New Invoice**.
2. Select a patient (Step 1), then add line items (Step 2).
3. Click **Save Invoice** → the invoice opens in **Draft** status.

### Step 2 — Review the Draft

- The invoice shows a **yellow banner** at the top: "Draft Invoice — Not Yet Issued".
- The invoice number shows as "DRAFT — not issued".
- You can navigate away and come back to review it before issuing.
- Draft invoices appear in the billing list with an "not yet issued" tag and are shown slightly dimmed.
- **The outstanding total and analytics do not include draft amounts.**

### Step 3 — Finalize the Invoice

When you are ready to issue the invoice to the patient:

1. Open the draft invoice.
2. Click the **Finalize Invoice** button in the yellow banner.
3. The status changes to **Issued** (green badge).
4. The invoice date is set to today.
5. The invoice number (e.g., INV-20260601-A1B2) appears.
6. The amount now counts toward outstanding receivables and revenue analytics.

> Once finalized, the invoice is locked. You cannot change line items.

### Step 4 — Record a Payment

After the invoice is finalized:

1. Open the invoice.
2. In the **Payment History** section, enter the amount and select mode (Cash / UPI / Card).
3. Click **Record**.
4. The status auto-updates to **Partially Paid** or **Paid**.
5. A green **Send Receipt via WhatsApp** button appears — click it to send the patient a payment confirmation.

> **Note:** You cannot record a payment on a Draft invoice. Finalize it first.

### Step 5 — Send WhatsApp Receipt

After recording a payment, the green WhatsApp button opens a pre-written message:

> *"Dear [Name], ₹[Amount] received via [Mode] at VEDA Dental. Invoice: INV-XXXX. Balance: ₹[Remaining]. Thank you! — 07660966674"*

Click the button, confirm in WhatsApp, and send.

---

### Quick reference — Invoice statuses

| Badge | Meaning | Counted in revenue? |
|-------|---------|---------------------|
| **Draft** (yellow) | Created, not issued | No |
| **Issued** (blue) | Finalized and sent | Yes — full amount is outstanding |
| **Partially Paid** (orange) | Some amount received | Yes — remaining is outstanding |
| **Paid** (green) | Fully settled | Yes — no outstanding |
| **Cancelled** (red) | Cancelled | No |
